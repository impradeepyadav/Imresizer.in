4

Printer Settings (Critical!)

These settings make the difference between professional and rejected photos:

Paper Size Select 4x6 inches (10x15 cm) or A4
Borderless Printing Enable "Borderless" or "Full Bleed" - CRITICAL!
Paper Type Select "Photo Paper" or "Glossy Photo Paper"
Print Quality Choose "Best" or "High Quality" (300 DPI)
Scale / Size Set to "Actual Size" or 100% - NO SCALING

⚠️ Common Mistake

If you don't enable borderless printing, you'll get white borders around your photos. The photos will be too small and may be rejected.

5

Print Test on Plain Paper First

Always do a test print:

  1. Load plain paper instead of photo paper
  2. Print one copy with same settings
  3. Check if photos are positioned correctly
  4. Measure one photo to verify size
  5. If good, proceed with photo paper

[Screenshot: Test print on plain paper with measurements]

📏 Always verify size before final print

6

Print Final Photos

  1. Load photo paper (glossy side up)
  2. Print using the settings above
  3. Let the print dry for 30-60 seconds
  4. Handle carefully - ink may smudge when wet
7

Cut Photos Perfectly

Cut along the guides for perfect-sized photos:

  • Scissors: Cut carefully along lines
  • Paper cutter: Best for straight, professional cuts
  • Ruler + craft knife: For precision cutting
✂️ Cutting Tip: Cut slightly inside the guide lines (1-2mm) to ensure photos meet exact size requirements. Better slightly smaller than slightly larger.

❌ Common Printing Problems & Solutions

Problem Solution
White borders around photos Enable "Borderless Printing" in printer settings
Photos too small or large Set scaling to "Actual Size" or 100%
Colors look wrong Check ink levels, run printer head cleaning
Paper jams Use recommended paper weight, fan sheets before loading
Ink smudges Let print dry longer, use proper paper type setting
Lines or streaks Clean printer heads, align cartridges

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will photo studios accept home-printed photos?

A: Yes, as long as they meet official size and quality requirements. Many people successfully use home-printed photos for passports, visas, and exams.

Q: What paper should I use?

A: Use 4x6 inch (10x15 cm) or A4 photo paper. Glossy gives a professional finish, matte is also acceptable. Avoid regular printer paper.

Q: Can I print on regular A4 paper?

A: You can, but it won't look professional. Photo paper is coated to produce sharp, vibrant images. Regular paper absorbs ink and looks dull.

Q: My prints have white borders. What's wrong?

A: Enable "Borderless Printing" in your printer settings. Also ensure you're not scaling the image.

Q: How long do home-printed photos last?

A: With proper storage and quality ink, they can last years. For official documents that need longevity, use archival quality photo paper.

Q: Can I print photos for multiple people on one sheet?

A: Yes! Our print generator allows mixing different photos on the same sheet. Perfect for family applications.

Q: What DPI should I use?

A> 300 DPI is standard for photo printing. Our tools automatically maintain this.
