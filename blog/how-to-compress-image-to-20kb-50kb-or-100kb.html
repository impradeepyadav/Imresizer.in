How to Compress Image to 20KB, 50KB, or 100KB

📅 March 1, 2026 • 📁 Image Tips • ⏱ 5 min read

In today's digital world, image size matters. Whether you are applying for a visa, filling out an online form, uploading a profile picture, or optimizing your website for speed, you often need to hit a specific file size limit.

But how do you reduce a 5MB photo down to a crisp 20KB, 50KB, or 100KB without ruining the quality?

Manually guessing with complex software like Photoshop can be tedious. Fortunately, with imresizer.in, you can compress any image to your exact target size in seconds. Here is your complete guide to getting the perfect file size.

Why Do You Need Specific Image Sizes?

Different platforms have different requirements:

  • Government Forms & Exams: Often require a passport-size photo under 20KB or 50KB.
  • E-commerce Portals: Product images might need to be under 100KB for fast loading.
  • Social Media: While they accept large files, smaller images (under 100KB) upload faster and save mobile data.
  • Website Performance: Google favors websites that load quickly. Compressing images to the smallest possible size boosts your SEO.

What Happens When You Compress an Image?

Compression reduces the file size by removing unnecessary data (lossy compression) or by reorganizing the data more efficiently (lossless).

  • To 100KB: Ideal for high-quality web images and detailed photos.
  • To 50KB: A sweet spot for medium-quality images that balance clarity and speed.
  • To 20KB: Perfect for thumbnails, icons, or strict form requirements where file size is the priority.

Tips for Compressing to Very Small Sizes (Like 20KB)

Reaching a size as low as 20KB can be tricky if you start with a huge image. Here are a few pro-tips to use on imresizer.in:

  1. Resize Dimensions First: If your image is 4000x3000 pixels, compressing it alone might not get it down to 20KB without turning it into a blurry mess. Use the Resize tool on imresizer.in first to lower the dimensions (e.g., to 500x500 pixels) and then compress it to 20KB.
  2. Start with JPG: For photographs, JPG format compresses much smaller than PNG.
  3. Check the Preview: Always preview the compressed image. If it looks too pixelated at 20KB, consider if you can meet a slightly higher limit like 30KB or 50KB for better clarity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I compress a PNG to 20KB?

A: Yes. However, PNG files are generally larger because they retain more detail. imresizer.in can handle PNGs, but for very small sizes like 20KB, you may get better results by converting to JPG during the process.

Q: Will the quality be bad at 50KB?

A: Not necessarily. For a small profile picture (say 300x300 pixels), 50KB can look very sharp. For a large banner image, 50KB might be too low. The key is to balance dimensions with file size.

Q: Is it free?
