📝 Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Create Your Passport Photo

Start with a compliant passport photo:

📱 Pro Tip: Take photo against a white wall with good lighting. Our tool will handle the rest!

Step 2: Choose Your Print Template

Use our print generator to arrange multiple copies:

  1. Select how many copies you need (2, 4, 6 per sheet)
  2. Choose your country's photo size (2x2 inch, 35x45mm, etc.)
  3. Preview the layout on 4x6 sheet
  4. Adjust spacing and margins if needed
  5. Download the print-ready sheet

Step 3: Printer Settings

Critical settings for perfect prints:

Paper Size 4x6 inches (10x15 cm) - Standard photo paper
Borderless Printing Enable "Borderless" or "Full Bleed" in printer settings
Paper Type Select "Photo Paper" or "Glossy Photo Paper"
Print Quality Choose "Best" or "High Quality" (300 DPI)
Scale / Size Set to "Actual Size" or 100% - no scaling

Step 4: Print & Cut

  1. Load 4x6 photo paper into your printer
  2. Print using the settings above
  3. Let the print dry for 30 seconds
  4. Use sharp scissors to cut along guides
  5. For perfect straight cuts, use a paper cutter
✂️ Cutting Tip: Use a ruler and craft knife for professional-looking straight edges. Cut slightly inside the guide lines to ensure photos meet exact size requirements.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will photo studios accept home-printed photos?

A: Yes, as long as they meet official size and quality requirements. Many people successfully use home-printed photos for passports, visas, and exams.

Q: What paper should I use?

A: Use 4x6 inch (10x15 cm) photo paper. Glossy gives a professional finish, but matte is also acceptable. Avoid regular printer paper as it looks unprofessional.

Q: Can I print on A4 paper?

A: Yes, but you'll waste space. 4x6 is the standard photo size and fits perfectly. If using A4, you can fit 8-10 photos per sheet.

Q: My prints have white borders. What's wrong?

A: Enable "Borderless Printing" in your printer settings. Also ensure you're not scaling the image.

Q: How long do home-printed photos last?

A: With proper storage and quality ink, they can last years. For official documents that need longevity, use archival quality photo paper.

Q: Can I print photos for multiple family members on one sheet?

A: Yes! Our print generator allows mixing different photos on the same sheet. Perfect for family applications.
