✅ Common Requirements Across All Exams

  • Photo Background: White or very light grey (no patterns, no shadows)
  • Photo Age: Taken within last 3-6 months (varies by exam)
  • Signature Ink: Use black pen on white paper
  • Expression: Neutral face, both eyes open, no smiling
  • Accessories: No hats, no sunglasses (prescription glasses allowed if no glare)
  • Format: Most exams accept only JPG/JPEG (some accept PNG)

❌ Top Reasons for Photo Rejection

Avoid These Mistakes

  1. Wrong file size: Photo over 50KB or under 10KB when specific limit is 20KB
  2. Incorrect dimensions: Not following exact cm or pixel requirements
  3. Wrong background: Using colored, patterned, or dark background
  4. Face covered: Hair over face, shadows, or turned head
  5. Old photo: Using photo that doesn't match current appearance
  6. Signature issues: Signature too small, too large, or on wrong background
  7. Format problems: Uploading PNG when JPG is required

📝 Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use the same photo for multiple exams?

A: Yes, but you may need to resize it differently for each exam's requirements. Save the original and create copies as needed.

Q: What if my photo is exactly 50KB but requirement says 20KB?

A: Use our Image Compressor with target 20KB. The tool will reduce size while maintaining quality.

Q: Is PNG accepted for government exams?

A: Most government exams specify JPG/JPEG only. Some like GATE accept PNG. Always check your exam's notification.

Q: How recent should my photo be?

A: Most exams require photos taken within the last 3-6 months. UPSC specifically mentions "within 30 days" for some applications.

Q: Can I wear spectacles in exam photos?

A: It's better to avoid if possible. If necessary, ensure no glare and frames don't cover eyes. Some exams explicitly prohibit glasses.

Q: What if my signature is rejected?

A: Common issues: too small, too large, or not matching name. Create a fresh signature using our Signature Maker with proper dimensions.
