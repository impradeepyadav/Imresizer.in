Passport Photo Requirements: Quick Reference

📅 February 25, 2026 • 📁 Passport • ⏱ 3 min read

A simple table of size and background requirements for USA, UK, India, Canada, China, and Schengen countries. No long paragraphs – just what you need.

📌 Quick Tips Before You Start:

  • Face must be clearly visible - no sunglasses, hats, or hair covering face
  • Recent photo only - taken within last 6 months
  • No edits or filters - natural appearance only
  • Good lighting - no shadows on face or background

✅ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Wrong background color - using grey when white is required
  • Smiling with teeth showing - neutral expression required for most countries
  • Head too small or too large - doesn't meet size percentage requirements
  • Glasses glare - remove glasses or use anti-glare coating
  • Shadows on face or background - use even lighting
  • Photo too old - must be within last 6 months

📝 Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I wear glasses in my passport photo?

A: Most countries now discourage or prohibit glasses in passport photos due to glare issues. USA requires no glasses. UK allows only if medically necessary with a signed note.

Q: What color background is best?

A: White is the safest choice for most countries. For UK and Schengen, light grey or cream is acceptable. Always check your specific country's requirements.

Q: Can I smile in my passport photo?

A: Most countries require a neutral expression with mouth closed and both eyes open. USA and UK require neutral expression with no smile.

Q: How recent should my photo be?

A: All countries require photos taken within the last 6 months to reflect your current appearance.
