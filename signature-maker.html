1. How to Create Signature by Drawing

The drawing method is the most natural way to create a signature that looks like your handwritten signature. Follow these steps:

  1. Select "Draw Signature" tab: This opens the drawing canvas.
  2. Adjust settings: Choose your preferred pen color, background color, and pen size using the sliders.
  3. Draw your signature: Use your mouse (on desktop) or finger/stylus (on mobile/touch screen) to draw directly on the canvas.
  4. Use controls: If you make a mistake, use "Undo" to remove the last stroke or "Clear All" to start fresh.
  5. Generate: Click "Generate Signature" when satisfied, then download as PNG or JPG.

✍️ Pro Tip for Drawing

For best results, use a stylus on mobile or draw slowly. Higher pen size values create thicker lines. If your signature looks jagged, increase the smoothing value for smoother curves.

2. How to Create Signature by Typing

Perfect for creating clean, professional signatures using various fonts:

  1. Select "Type Signature" tab: This opens the text input panel.
  2. Enter your name: Type your full name as you want it to appear in the signature.
  3. Choose font style: Select from 10+ professionally curated fonts including elegant scripts (Brush Script, Pacifico), formal serifs (Times New Roman, Georgia), and clean sans-serifs (Arial, Verdana).
  4. Adjust size and color: Pick the appropriate font size and text color.
  5. Preview: See real-time preview of your signature on the canvas below.
  6. Generate: Click "Generate & Save" to create your signature.

🔤 Font Selection Tips

For official documents, choose professional fonts like Arial, Times New Roman, or Georgia. For a personal touch, Brush Script or Pacifico mimics handwriting beautifully. Test different sizes to ensure your signature fits well in document fields.

3. How to Create Signature by Uploading

Already have a scanned image of your signature? Upload it and convert to a clean digital signature:

  1. Select "Upload Signature" tab: This opens the upload area.
  2. Upload image: Click the upload area or drag & drop your signature image (PNG, JPG, GIF).
  3. Automatic processing: The tool automatically centers and resizes your signature to fit the canvas.
  4. Review: Check if the signature looks good. You can remove and re-upload if needed.
  5. Save: Click "Save as Signature" to process and download.

📸 Upload Best Practices

For best results, upload an image with a clean white background and dark ink. The tool works best with high-contrast images. If your signature appears too light, try a different source image.

4. Download Options: PNG vs JPG

After creating your signature, you have two download formats:

  • PNG (Portable Network Graphics): Supports transparent background. Ideal for overlaying on documents, PDFs, or any colored background. The signature appears without any white box around it. Recommended for most professional uses.
  • JPG (JPEG): Always has a white background. Smaller file size but lacks transparency. Suitable for basic use where background doesn't matter.

Recommendation: Always choose PNG for official documents to maintain a professional look without background artifacts.

Q: Can I use this signature for legal documents?

A: Yes, digital signatures created with our tool are widely accepted for most online forms, applications, and digital documents. Q: What file format is best for signatures?

A: PNG format is recommended as it supports transparency, allowing your signature to be placed on any colored background without a white box. JPG format adds a white background and is smaller in file size but less versatile.

9. Tips for Perfect Digital Signature

  • Practice drawing: If using draw mode, practice a few times to get comfortable with the mouse or touch input.
  • Choose appropriate size: For most forms, a signature around 200-300 pixels wide is sufficient.
  • Maintain consistency: Use the same signature for all your documents to maintain consistency.
